1. Introduction

Flooding remains one of the most destructive natural hazards in West Africa, with impacts expected to intensify under climate change. This study analyzes rainfall-runoff interactions, terrain storage, and population exposure across the Mono River Basin using remote sensing datasets.

Objective: Evaluate flood-prone areas based on rainfall, land use, population, and built infrastructure, and identify key risk zones.

4. Key Findings

  • High flood frequency occurs in lowland sub-basins along the main Mono River channel.
  • Urban centers near the river show the highest population and building exposure.
  • Seasonal rainfall peaks (June‚ÄìSeptember) correspond to the highest modeled flood extents.
  • The derived risk map aligns with documented flood-affected zones in southern Togo and Benin.
