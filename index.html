Flood Impact Prediction in the Mono River Basin

Identifying flood-prone areas in the Mono River Basin using rainfall-runoff simulation and geospatial analysis.

1. Introduction

Flooding remains one of the most destructive natural hazards in West Africa, with impacts expected to intensify under climate change. This project evaluates flood risk in the Mono River Basin using satellite-derived rainfall and terrain data. By simulating runoff and water accumulation using a volume comparison method, this project identifies areas historically vulnerable to flooding and evaluates how infrastructure and population may be affected.

Although this analysis is based on historical rainfall, the workflow is designed as a foundation for future predictive tools. With real-time rainfall input or climate projections, this method can be extended into an early warning or planning system.

Objective: Evaluate flood-prone areas based on rainfall input and built infrastructure and population.

3.3 Flood Extent Estimation – Volume Comparison Method

Runoff coefficient is derived from landuse layer.
For each monthly rainfall raster:
RunoffVolume (m³) = (Rainfall_mm / 1000) × RunoffCoefficient × PixelArea (m²)
For each pixel in the watershed: runoff volume is compared against storage capacity (assumed 100 mm).

Flooded area is defined where: runoff_volume > capacity_threshold

3.4 Risk Index Calculation

  • Exposure_Index = 0.7 × Norm_Pop + 0.3 × Norm_Buildings
  • Vulnerability_Index = 0.7 × LandCoverScore + 0.3 × NormalizedPopulation
  • Hazard_Index = 0.5 × NormalizedDepth + 0.5 × NormalizedFrequency
  • Risk_Index = 0.5 × Hazard + 0.3 × Exposure + 0.2 × Vulnerability

4.3 Key Findings

  • High flood frequency occurs in lowland sub-basins.
  • Rainfall-driven flood peaks occur from June to September.
  • Urban and densely populated zones coincide with highest risk levels.
  • Risk index aligns well with historically affected areas in Togo and Benin.
